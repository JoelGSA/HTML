Inclusive customer service LMS — three-module course breakdown

Inclusive customer service

The Inclusive Customer Service course provides a contemporary understanding of disability, accessibility, and inclusion, and addresses barriers for people with disability in everyday life. The course reinforces learnings through the use of supporting videos and quizzes.

Module 1
Understanding disability, accessibility, and inclusion

In this module, you will explore what disability is and develop a contemporary understanding of how disability can impact a person's ability to participate fully and equally in everyday life. You'll learn about the different models of disability and examine the concepts of accessibility and inclusion. This module includes a video and an end-of-module quiz to reinforce your learning.

  • Understanding disability
  • Models of disability
  • Understanding accessibility
  • Inclusion in practice
Module 2
Inclusive communication

This module explores the importance of inclusive language and why it matters when communicating with people with disability. It covers the use of clear, respectful, and accessible communication, including alternative communication methods. You'll also gain practical tools and real-world examples to help ensure your interactions are inclusive, respectful, and person-centred.

  • Why inclusive language matters
  • Tips for inclusive communication
  • Alternate communication methods
  • Inclusive conversation checklist
Module 3
Inclusive customer service

In this final module, you'll learn about the diverse access and communication requirements that customers may have. This includes practical guidance for interacting with people who have a psychological disability, are Deaf or hard of hearing, are blind or have low vision, or have physical or mobility limitations. The module also explores why understanding and responding to each person's individual preferences is essential to delivering an inclusive and positive customer service experience.

  • Understanding different access requirements
  • Tips for communicating with customers with different disability types (e.g. deaf or hard of hearing, blind or low vision, neurodiversity, physical disability and low mobility)
